Travel

Travelling has always been a great passion of mine. I love the feeling of arriving in new places, with different, unique cultures to explore and learn about. This admiration for other nations probably stems from my time living in Brussels. We moved there when I was young, and it gave me the oppurtunity to take in many new perspectives and viewpoints which I may never have discovered had we not moved. Attending a European School, I was surrounded by students of many different nationalities and backgrounds, which actually helped me to integrate into the school faster than I would have otherwise, as I realised that I was not the only one who wasn't a native born Belgian.

Additionally, due to this experience, I was motivated to visit other countries and experience new customs. One of my travels that particularly sticks out for me was when I visited Venice. Without a doubt the most magnificent city I've ever seen. On the surface, it may seem a bit run-down. However, a closer look reveals one of the most awe-inspiring feets of architecture ever achieved. The sheer brilliance that the clear canals bring out in the city is mistifying, and cannot be overstated. For the fact that I was able to visit Venice alone, I am grateful for the experience I was given by living abroad.
